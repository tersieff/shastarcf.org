Financials

Shasta Regional Community Foundation encourages the local investment of local resources. We manage this philanthropic capital to provide the highest level of earnings and benefit—to our donors and to the charitable causes they support. As an authorized public charity, Shasta Regional Community Foundation makes financial reporting and investment practices available to the public.

2013 Financial Facts

  • Net investment assets: $21.1 million
  • Endowed permanent assets: $15.6 million
  • Annual grants: $1,385,000 awarded in grants in 2012
  • Cumulative grants: More than $11.9 million since 2000
  • Charitable funds: 102

Investments

Shasta Regional Community Foundation’s funds are professionally managed for growth over time and overseen by our volunteer Board of Directors and Investment Advisory Committee. Annual audit reports are available by request.

Objectives

  • Stability of principal
  • High current income
  • Current income with potential growth of income and capital
  • Maximum total return from capital appreciation and income
  • Maximum capital appreciation

Performance

Donors receive semi-annual statements detailing the balance of their short-termed or endowed funds, including contributions, grants and investment activity for the fiscal year-to-date. The Community Foundation’s fiscal year is July 1 – June 30.

Donors may contact the Community Foundation for performance summaries.

Fees charged

To support management of donated charitable assets, we charge administrative contributions based on the fair market value of funds being managed. Read our Administrative Fee Schedule (514KB PDF) for a summary of our fees.
